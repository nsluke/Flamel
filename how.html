Mercury Procurement Not Started

Every great product begins with raw materials. Ours begins with mercury-198 (198Hg), a stable isotope that comprises approximately 9.97% of naturally occurring mercury. The other ~90% of mercury is, for our purposes, useless contaminant that will eventually make our gold radioactive. But we'll get to that.

Isolating pure 198Hg from mixed-isotope mercury requires industrial-scale isotope separation — a process so expensive and energy-intensive that it is primarily performed by national governments for nuclear weapons programs. We plan to do it on a startup budget.

Our current procurement strategy is best described as "optimistic networking." Our CEO attended a minerals conference in Dubai where she exchanged business cards with someone who she believes works in isotope enrichment. The business card was in a language she doesn't read. She remains confident.

* Flamel does not currently possess any mercury, enriched or otherwise. The Minamata Convention on Mercury (2017) restricts international mercury trade. We are choosing to view this as a "regulatory moat."

Fusion Transmutation Theoretical

The heart of Flamel's technology is a deuterium-tritium (D-T) tokamak fusion reactor modified with our proprietary mercury-198 transmutation blanket. In a standard D-T reaction, hydrogen isotopes fuse at approximately 150 million degrees Celsius — roughly ten times the temperature of the Sun's core — producing helium, clean energy, and critically, high-energy neutrons at 14 MeV.

In conventional fusion designs, these neutrons are captured by a lithium blanket to breed tritium fuel. Our innovation is replacing this blanket with 198Hg, which undergoes an (n,2n) reaction when struck by neutrons above 6 MeV. This ejects a neutron from the mercury nucleus, creating unstable 197Hg, which decays via electron capture (half-life: ~64 hours) into stable gold-197 — the only naturally occurring isotope of gold.

It's an elegant piece of nuclear physics. It's also entirely hypothetical at our scale. No commercial fusion reactor has ever achieved sustained net-energy-positive operation, anywhere on Earth, ever. The global fusion industry has spent approximately $60 billion over 70 years attempting to solve this problem. Our Series A target is $12 million.

Our CTO has built a "digital twin" of the reactor in Blender. It looks incredible. It does not simulate physics, but the lighting is very realistic and it renders in real-time at 60fps, which is more than most tokamaks can say.

* Flamel does not possess a fusion reactor, tritium fuel, superconducting magnets, a cryogenic cooling system, a building, or the approximately $20-40 billion required to build a commercial tokamak. We do have the Blender file.

03

Radioactive Sequestration Vibes Only

Here's the part we'd rather not lead with on the pitch deck.

Because our mercury feedstock will inevitably contain mixed isotopes (see: Stage 1, "the isotope separation we can't afford"), the neutron bombardment will produce not only beautiful, stable 197Au but also a chaotic assortment of radioactive gold isotopes. The raw gold extracted from the reactor will be, in a word, "spicy."

This gold cannot be worn as jewelry (radiation burns). It cannot be used in electronics (catastrophic bit-flips). It cannot be held by central banks (OSHA violations). It cannot be touched by human hands or, ideally, looked at directly without shielding.

To comply with international radiological safety standards, the transmuted gold must be placed in a heavily shielded sequestration facility — think meters of high-density concrete, lead glass viewing windows, and robotic manipulator arms — for a mandatory cooling period of 14 to 18 years while the radioactive isotopes decay into stable elements.

We refer to this phase internally as "the maturation period," because "nearly two decades of mandatory radioactive storage before we can recognize any revenue" tested poorly with investors. Our CFO's financial model handles this gracefully: the revenue column simply reads "$0" for the first 18 rows, followed by a hockey stick so aggressive it required a custom Y-axis.

* Flamel does not possess a sequestration vault, shielding materials, hot cell infrastructure, robotic manipulator arms, lead glass, or a site zoned for radioactive material storage. We have identified a promising abandoned warehouse on Google Maps. Zoning TBD.

Certification & Delivery 2044 (Optimistic)

After approximately 18 years of quiet radioactive decay, our gold will finally achieve the isotopic purity required to be legally classified as safe for human contact. At this point, every single ounce must undergo rigorous independent certification proving it is completely free of hazardous trace isotopes like 195Au and residual 194Hg.

This certification framework does not currently exist. No regulatory body has ever needed to create standards for "Radiologically Cleared Fusion Gold" because no one has ever been foolish enough to try to sell it. We view this regulatory vacuum not as a barrier, but as an opportunity to write the rules ourselves, which we're sure the NRC will be thrilled about.

Once certified, the gold enters the global commodity market as investment-grade bullion. We anticipate that consumers will be initially hesitant to purchase gold that spent two decades in a radioactive containment facility. Our marketing team (Zephyr) has proposed branding it as "Atomic Gold™" with the tagline "Forged in Fusion. Aged in Lead." Focus groups have not been conducted because we don't have focus groups, or gold, or a marketing budget.
