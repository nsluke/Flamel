Dr. Aurelia Mercurius
Co-Founder, CEO & Chief Alchemist

Aurelia's journey into chrysopoeia began on a long-haul flight when she accidentally clicked on a YouTube video titled "CERN Scientists Turn Lead Into Gold (REAL)" instead of the in-flight movie. By the time the plane landed, she had drafted a 47-slide pitch deck on her phone and emailed her two-weeks notice to McKinsey from the tarmac.

Prior to founding Flamel, Aurelia spent 2.5 years at McKinsey & Company where she specialized in "transformation" — a word she has since taken extremely literally. Her engagement history includes advising a Fortune 500 mining company on "digital synergies," which she describes as "basically the same thing as nuclear transmutation but for org charts."

Aurelia holds a partially completed MBA from a program she describes as "prestigious but ultimately too slow for the pace of atomic innovation." She was asked to leave after submitting a capstone project proposing that Goldman Sachs pivot to literal gold synthesis. The professor called it "the most confidently wrong thing I've ever read." She took this as validation.

"People said alchemy was impossible. People also said Uber was impossible. One of those people was right, and I choose to believe it's neither."

"Aurelia is without question the most ambitious person I have ever managed. I cannot in good conscience say anything else about this venture."

Ptolemy Vandenberg III
Co-Founder, CTO & Neutron Whisperer

Ptolemy holds a PhD in Computational Materials Science from a university he prefers to describe as "European." His doctoral thesis, "Stochastic Modeling of Hypothetical Neutron Flux Distributions in Non-Existent Reactor Geometries," was praised by his advisor as "technically correct in the same way that a map of Narnia is technically cartography."

Before Flamel, Ptolemy completed a summer internship at CERN, where he was responsible for monitoring a temperature gauge on a piece of equipment adjacent to (but not part of) the Large Hadron Collider. He describes this experience as "hands-on particle physics." His LinkedIn lists his role as "Nuclear Research Associate, CERN" which is, by the most generous possible interpretation, not entirely false.

As CTO, Ptolemy has architected Flamel's entire technology stack, which currently consists of a Kubernetes cluster running a real-time dashboard that displays the word "TRANSMUTING..." in gold text with a loading spinner. The cluster costs $4,200/month in cloud compute. When asked what it's computing, he replies "the future." He has also built a proprietary "digital twin" of the fusion reactor, which is a 3D model he made in Blender over a weekend. It does not simulate physics.

"The difference between a particle accelerator and a SaaS platform is really just a matter of abstraction. Both involve moving things very fast through a pipeline."
LinkedIn Endorsement — PhD Thesis Advisor
"Ptolemy has a remarkable ability to connect concepts that have no business being connected. I mean this descriptively, not prescriptively."

Cassandra Ledger
Co-Founder, CFO & Chief Copium Officer

Cassandra is a financial modeling savant whose career is best described as a series of increasingly creative interpretations of the phrase "path to profitability." Before Flamel, she served as CFO at three consecutive startups: a direct-to-consumer luxury water brand ("AquaLux," dissolved), a blockchain-based timeshare platform ("ChainStay," dissolved), and a subscription service for artisanal ice ("FrostCraft," dissolved, ironically).

At Flamel, Cassandra has constructed what she calls "the most sophisticated financial model in nuclear history" — a 847-tab Excel workbook that projects revenue beginning in fiscal year 2044. The model includes 312 sensitivity analyses, each of which returns a negative NPV. Cassandra has annotated every single one with the note "conservative estimate." She does not use Google Sheets because "you can't build a serious reactor on someone else's infrastructure."

Her proudest professional achievement is a DCF model so long that Excel crashes when you scroll to the terminal value. She considers this a feature. "If the spreadsheet can't contain the upside, neither can the market."

"Every great company was pre-revenue once. We're just pre-revenue for a longer, more nuclear period of time."
LinkedIn Endorsement — Former AquaLux Board Member
"Cassandra has a gift for making the financially impossible feel just barely plausible. She did it for water, she did it for ice, and I have no doubt she'll do it for radioactive gold."

Zephyr Goldstein
Co-Founder, Head of Vibes & Isotope Relations

Zephyr's path to nuclear physics was unconventional, in that it did not involve nuclear physics at any point. After completing a BA in Art History with a minor in "Experiential Wellness" from a liberal arts college in Vermont, Zephyr spent four years as a barista at a specialty coffee shop where they developed what they call "an intuitive understanding of molecular transformation" — referring to the Maillard reaction in espresso roasting, which they insist is "basically the same energy" as nuclear transmutation.

At Flamel, Zephyr's role defies traditional corporate taxonomy. They manage "stakeholder relationships with unstable isotopes," which in practice means they have assigned names and personalities to each of the 40 known radioactive gold isotopes. Gold-198 is "Gregory." Gold-195 is "a little dramatic but means well." They maintain a Notion board tracking the "emotional wellbeing" of each isotope and have requested budget for a "isotope team offsite" that HR (also Zephyr) has approved.

Zephyr also leads Flamel's brand and culture initiatives. They designed the company's meditation room (a closet with a Himalayan salt lamp), authored the internal values document ("The Seven Noble Truths of Transmutation"), and host a weekly all-hands they call "Gold Circle" where the team shares gratitude and reviews burn rate. Attendance is mandatory. Snacks are not provided.

"Gold isn't just an element. Gold is a feeling. And feelings can't be regulated by the NRC. I checked."
LinkedIn Endorsement — Barista Colleague (2019)
"Zephyr made the best oat milk lattes I've ever had. I don't understand what they're doing now but they seem very happy about it."
